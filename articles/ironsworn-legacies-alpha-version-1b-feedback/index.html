Ironsworn Legacies Alpha Version 1b Feedback

This is my response to the questionnaire for the Alpha Version 1b playtest of Ironsworn: Legacies.

Did you play solo, co-op, or guided?

Solo. I played a sort of two-shot of a single quest. I am not sure if I want to continue that into playing longer. I may wait for the next version, or try playing with someone else first.

What was fun?

Most of what I enjoyed I also enjoy out of Ironsworn and Starforged (the interplay between mechanics and narrative, the freeform-ness of it, etc). It doesn't seem too fair to point to those things as something Ironsworn: Legacies uniquely does that's fun.

Resources are fun enough that I want to backport them, or a very similar system, into Ironsworn (assuming that's no problem with Shawn).

What was un-fun?

The Take Action move in general. Ironsworn Legacies replaces a flowchart for "what move to use" with a single move that takes a flowchart to use. It's an identical move and identical system no matter what the context or situation I'm in. I knew that no matter what I was doing or trying, I'd be going through the Take Action checklist and allocating Good Boy/Bad Boy points for the dozenth time. I didn't want to keep doing that anymore. It felt tedious and boring and kept throwing me out of play while I did the cognitive workflow of adding up misfortunes/advantages and shopping with them.

Position is a win more/lose more mechanic that is largely luck-driven and there's very few ways to steer it. It's not fun to pay the costs of a miss and be told I'm going to keep suffering for it for the forseeable future. Meanwhile, I don't feel like I did anything "right" except roll better to be in a favored position and earn more advantages. Tempting fate amplified this, and it all felt very swingy back-and-forth and unstable.

I find myself uncomfortable with the Legacy system and I'm not sure I have the full vocabulary to explain why. I would probably be more comfortable talking about this in a DM than in a public play report test, but the theming and framing of how Legacies is shaping up I am not sure sits well with me.

What confused you?

It's not clear if finding an opportunity is permanent or temporary Favored. I went with permanent based on the wording.

I'm confused over if I'm supposed to "pre-buy" advantages/misfortunes before rolling or not. The Codexes seem to imply what I'm doing to justify a Take Action roll dictates what advantages I get, but if I didn't look at the codexes, I would be left to my own devices on what mechanical outcomes my action granted me (and then fitting them into the story). At the same time the misfortunes/advantages in the codex are about what happens after my character does the thing, so, I dunno, it's like a chicken-and-egg Klein bottle. Am I supposed to have an outcome in mind before rolling or not?

"Resource" I think is a little too close to "Supply" as far as wording goes even if they're extremely different things. It's Ironsworn and Starforged in me talking, but "acquiring resources" to me would be something related to a Supply track that doesn't exist in this game. There is something about the keywording in the core rulebook that feels off to me in general, but I don't know how to define that more concretely.

What was missing (keeping in mind the incomplete nature of the materials)?

If we're supposed to start with 3 assets, then we're playtesting with intentionally underpowered characters. It is probably no wonder then that people are falling into doom spirals: we're not being given the bonuses we're "expected" to have.

Were the random tables in the codexes useful and inspirational?

I am not really an oracle roller in my play. I gave them a skim and a few rolls to see. I don't have too strong of an opinion on them, although they seem to have a little too much play advice to be considered 100% optional.

Were the outcomes too harsh for your character, too easy, or about right?

I'm not entirely sure. I mostly know it felt very frustrating in way I don't really feel in Ironsworn/Starforged except for the deepest miss spirals. I think some of it comes from that I couldn't "choose" my own consequences: I was given a concrete number of Good Boy/Bad Boy points that I had to distribute.

How many boons did you acquire? Let’s call this Boons per Hour (BPH).

5 boons. I could not tell you what that is in Boons per Hour.

How many impacts did you mark? Did you mark severe (or greater) impacts?

Several, but I didn't keep a full tally of them. I inflicted and recovered from them liberally. I marked a severe one for my Supply.

Did you mend impacts? How?

Yes, through Take Action.

Did you burn momentum?

I never had a situation where I could.

Did you choose to Pass Time?

Not in play, although I went through the process once I was done.
